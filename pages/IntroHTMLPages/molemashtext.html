Getting a Mole to Appear

To begin, Create a new project and drag in a canvas (under Drawing and Animation). The Canvas component always comes in way too small, so lets change its width property to fill parent and its height property to 300 pixels.

Now that we have the place where our Mole is supposed to appear and disappear, let's get a mole on the Canvas. The component you should use for this is the Image Sprite component (also found under Drawing and Animation).

If you test your app after dragging in the image sprite, you will not see an image sprite because there is no image for your mole. Upload a (small) image into your app and set your imagesprite1 picture to the image in the properties in the designer. If the picture is too big, either resize it using your computer software, or change the imagesprite size properties (width and height) to be a small square (ex 30 x30).

Getting a Mole to Appear In Random Places On The Screen

In the Designer, drag in a Clock Component under the Sensors Drawer and lets take a look at it’s properties.

TimerAlwaysFires means that the Clock is on a loop. A loop means that once it counts down from 1000 ms and gets to 0, it will begin at 1000 ms and begin to count down again.

TimerEnabled means that the timer will count down.

TimerInterval is the number, in Miliseconds, that will be counted down from.

In the blocks editor, the Clock1.Timer eventhandler will be executed each time the timer gets to 0.
